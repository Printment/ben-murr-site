Building a personal website from scratch as a Head of Product

What started as a desire for a sharper personal website became a practical lesson in design systems, Git, deployment, domains, and what changes when a PM gets close enough to the work to ship it.

I wanted this site to do two jobs at once. First, it needed to present me credibly as a product leader. Second, it needed to reflect the fact that I’m increasingly interested in doing more of the work myself, especially where AI makes that possible.

That combination quickly turned the project into more than a branding exercise. It became a practical test of whether I could move beyond directing work and get closer to design, code, Git, deployment, and the kind of technical detail I’d usually rely on engineers to handle.

Why this mattered

There is a difference between understanding a workflow in theory and understanding it through delivery. Setting up GitHub, connecting Vercel, handling DNS, and shaping a design system all forced me into the mechanics. That made the learning stick.

It also changed how I think about AI-enabled product work. The value is not only speed. It is the expansion of who can get meaningfully involved in execution, provided they are willing to learn the systems underneath the surface.

What the build involved

The process broke down into a few practical layers: visual direction, component design, static page structure, Git setup, GitHub publishing, Vercel deployment, and finally custom-domain configuration through IONOS.

None of those parts were impossible individually. The value came from doing them end to end and seeing how design decisions, content structure, and technical delivery all connect.

The real learning came from shipping the whole chain, not from understanding each part in isolation.

What I would carry forward

The next step is not to turn into a full-time engineer. It is to keep building enough familiarity that technical work becomes less abstract, more discussable, and more testable in my own hands.

That is the broader theme of this blog. Not hobby tinkering for its own sake, but practical experimentation that makes me a stronger product leader and a more credible operator in AI-heavy environments.
